## Demo Flow

1. Start on the title screen and give a short pitch: a 2D arcade space shooter with waves, leaders, powerups, and audio feedback (30 seconds).
2. Show menu navigation, the controls screen, and one accessibility option such as reduced flash or mute (30 seconds).
3. Start gameplay and demonstrate the basic loop: movement, shooting, enemies, scoring, and HUD readability (1 minutes).
4. Show one powerup pickup and briefly point out the feedback cues on the HUD and the screen (1 minute).
5. Reach a later wave or leader phase to show the defense line, leader activation, and boss bar (1–2 minutes).
6. Explain one important iteration example: how the original grid-like waves were replaced with a more varied wave flow and stronger level pacing (1 minutes).
7. Share one postmortem highlight: the visual pass made the game feel more complete, and one thing we would change is doing machine dry-runs earlier (1 minute).
8. Close with either a victory or game over screen, mention the score saving feature, then answer questions (1–2 minutes).

## Best moments To Show

1. A wave entering the field and creating immediate pressure
2. A rapid fire powerup pickup with defeating a wave, visible HUD and screen feedback
3. A leader encounter with the defense line and boss health bar

## Backup plan
If the game crashes during the demo:

- Keep a short gameplay recording ready on a laptop or phone
- Keep screenshots of the title screen, gameplay HUD, leader encounter, and game over / victory screens
- If the game crashes, explain what should happen next and continue the demo using the recording or screenshots
- If audio fails, continue with live gameplay and play sounds designed for cues and gameplay action.

## Time management

- Intro and pitch: 30 seconds
- Menus and controls: 30–45 seconds
- Core gameplay: 2 minutes
- Leader or highlight moment: 1–2 minutes
- Iteration story: 1–2 minutes
- Postmortem takeaway and Q&A: 1–2 minutes
